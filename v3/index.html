Honest Talk: This Isn't For Everyone

There are two types of people who won't succeed with this, and I'd rather save your time upfront.

The IKEA Test

You know the IKEA cabinet that comes in a box with bags of screws, a metal screwdriver, and illustrated instructions? Ask yourself honestly: are you the type who tosses the instructions and jumps right in? Or do you follow the steps?

Tax defaulted property investing requires following a process: searching for specific criteria online, evaluating properties systematically, and executing deals step by step. If you can follow instructions, you can do this. If you're the type who assumes you already know everything, this won't work for you.

Just like IKEA, you don't need to follow instructions, but 8 out of 10 cabinets you put together will fall apart. This business is the same. Without what I teach, you might make one deal out of 5 work, but the losses of the other four deals will kill you.

If you're still reading, good. You have the right temperament for this.

The Greed Test

I teach students to make a quick, good, safe, but modest, profit. Here's what that looks like:

You might pick up a property for $7,000. It has no mortgage. The market value might be $110,000. I teach my students to resell it quickly for around $47,000, well below retail, but still a $30,000+ profit after expenses. You do this 4-5 times a year, spending about 7-10 hours a week.

The key insight: my students let the next buyer take the risk of finding a retail purchaser, dealing with mortgages, and managing tenants. My students buy low and sell low. Quick in, quick out, predictable profits.

If you're looking to get rich on a single deal, this isn't for you. If you're looking for a repeatable system that generates real cash flow, keep reading.
